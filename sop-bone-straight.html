Industrial Standard SOP-BNS-2024

Bone-Straight Hair Processing

Precision Reduction, Thermal Tension & Chemical Oxidation

Safety Protocol Required

Handling 50% Hydrogen Peroxide requires nitrile gloves and splash goggles. In case of skin contact, flush with water immediately. High-volume ventilation is mandatory during the Ammonia titration phase.

PHASE I: The Reduction

Disulfide Bond Depolymerization

01

Saturation & Immersion

Fully saturate the loose hair bundles with 150ml of prepared Solution A. For high-density wefts, use a padding motion to force solution into the medulla. Maintain temperature at 25°C.

02

TGA Reduction Timing

Dwell for 25–40 minutes depending on hair cuticle thickness. Perform a stretch test every 5 minutes after the 20-minute mark.

PHASE II: Thermal Alignment

Mechanical Structural Reconstruction

03

Neutral Rinse & Moisture Extraction

Rinse for 5 minutes with tepid water (pH 7.0). Blow-dry to 100% dry under linear tension using a high-tension ceramic comb.

04

Ceramic Sealing (210°C)

Pass the titanium flat iron 12 to 15 times per 1cm section. Use slow, consistent passes to permanently realign the protein chains in a flat configuration.

PHASE III: Chemical Oxidation

Molecular Fixation & Relocking

05

Locking Saturation (4% H₂O₂)

Apply 150ml of prepared Solution B. This re-oxidizes the disulfide bonds in their new straight state. Dwell for exactly 12 minutes.

PHASE IV: Post-Processing

Neutralization & Structural Cleansing

06

Neutralizing Wash

Rinse Solution B for 10 minutes with lukewarm water. Wash twice with an acid-balanced (pH 4.5–5.5) professional shampoo to remove all peroxide traces.

07

Deep Fiber Recovery

Apply a moisture-rich deep conditioner. Comb through and dwell for 5 minutes. Rinse well and apply a heat-protectant serum before the final dry.

08

Final Thermal Sealing

Blow-dry the hair completely. Perform a Final Precision Pass with the flat iron at 180°C to seal the cuticle and reveal the "Bone-Straight" glass finish.
